##### [a letter received by The Manhattan Company]

#### January 4th, 1900

My dear Mr. Thatcher, 


With the dawn of an exciting new century, I am premiering an entirely new act. The elements of this show are strictly under wraps, as any number of my rival magicians would wish to poach the ideas from me, but I would happily explain the entire idea of the thing in person, at your earliest convenience. Rest assured, it is unlike anything that has graced the stages of New York—or anywhere else—before. 

I’d ask that you’d indulge me in sharing a meal so that I may tell you of my plans and discuss potential financing opportunities. My hope is that, now that I have paid back previous loans, we may start anew, along with the century. 

I hope you have a happy new year and I am, 

Yours sincerely,

J. S. Fogg

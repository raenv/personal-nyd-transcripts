##### [A restaurant in one of Chicago’s finest hotels. The lunch crowd lingers into cocktail hour] 

#### JACK   

_[muttering]_ Where is he... _[annoyed sigh]_ 

##### [The door to the cafe opens, quick footsteps in]

#### JACK  

There you are 

##### [The footsteps approach the table]

#### CHARLIE  

I know, I know, I’m late-- 

#### JACK 

Four hours late. 

##### [The footsteps stop]

#### CHARLIE    I know. I’m sorry. 

##### [The two men shake hands]

How are you, John? 

#### JACK  

Quite honestly, ready to move from tea to bourbon, given we are nearly at cocktail hour. 

##### [Charles sits down]

#### CHARLIE 

I really do apologize. 

#### JACK   

I’m mostly poking fun--I’m just surprised, that’s all. It isn’t like you to be late. 

#### CHARLIE   

I know. 

#### JACK   

Is everything alright? I half thought you’d finally perished somewhere. 

#### CHARLIE   

Oh no, nothing as dramatic as all that. I was waylaid at the bank--

#### JACK  

Counting all of your riches? 

#### CHARLIE   

Figuring out how to give them away, in fact. 

#### JACK   

What on earth do you mean? 

##### [Footsteps of a waiter approaching]

#### CHARLIE  

Well 

#### JACK  

Would you like something to drink? 

#### CHARLIE  

Oh, um, yes- a brandy and soda, I suppose. 

#### JACK   

Make that two 

##### [The waiter walks away]

Apologies, I caught his eye 

#### CHARLIE  

No, it’s good, I could use a bracer. 

#### JACK  

Because you’re making yourself destitute? 

#### CHARLIE

_[scoffs]_ I think the price of gold would have to plummet for that to happen--no, I’m simply looking for ways to offload some of the wealth, now that I have so much of it. 

#### JACK   

Be it ever so humble. 

#### CHARLIE   

I’m not boasting, it’s simply the truth. The market has been shockingly good to me ever since the war and my accounts were hardly bare before then. Besides, you can’t exactly cast stones--I can’t get a ticket at any of your theaters. 

#### JACK 

You’re acquainted with the proprietor, I’m sure he could arrange something if there was a show you wanted to see. 

#### CHARLIE  

You know that isn’t what I mean.

#### JACK  

Yes, the entertainment trade is quite good. The desire for joy and frivolity hasn’t faded since the war and I plan on riding that particular horse all the way to the finishing post. 

#### CHARLIE  

And what are you doing with your spoils? Buying more theaters? 

#### JACK 

To start with. But now I feel I’m quite full up and thought it might be interesting to get into the moving pictures business. 

#### CHARLIE  

Hm. It does seem to be sticking around, doesn’t it? 

#### JACK   

You don’t approve? 

#### CHARLIE  

It isn’t a matter of approving or not approving--I think there are quite a few real talents out there actually. 

#### JACK  

You just have a little fancy for Clara Bow. 

#### CHARLIE  

I have no such thing. 

#### JACK  

Oh, “no such”--I remember when we went to see “Maytime” and that wasn’t how it seemed. You couldn’t take your eyes of her. 

#### CHARLIE  

I couldn’t take my eyes off the screen. It seemed so much like magic, except I couldn’t work out the trick. 

#### JACK  

And now that you understand how it works? 

#### CHARLIE  

How do you know I understand how it works? 

#### JACK  

Please, Charles, I’ve known you for nearly fifty years, I know that the instant you left that picture house, you dedicated yourself to learning everything about it. 

#### CHARLIE  

I always have been so predictable to you, haven’t I? How perfectly awful for you, to be stuck in immortality with the one man who will never be able to surprise you. 

#### JACK  

I wouldn’t say that. You surprised me today, after all, what with being so tardy. 

#### CHARLIE   

I see how it’s going to be--this is the next thirteen years, isn’t it? Being teased and reprimanded for a single lapse in my otherwise stalwart punctuality.

#### JACK    

_[a small laugh]_ I promise I’ll have it out of my system by the end of the night. I think. 

##### [The waiter comes back with their drinks]

_[to the waiter]_ Thank you. 

##### [Waiter walks off]

This certainly helps. 

#### CHARLIE   

Then a toast to your swift forgiveness. 

#### JACK   

Cheers. 

##### [They cheers]

So how are you finding Chicago? 

#### CHARLIE  

It’s hardly my first time, as you well know. 

#### JACK  

It’s been a while though. I was surprised you were willing to meet me here.

#### CHARLIE  

I’m not the one who hates the cold. 

#### JACK  

Oh and it is dreadful here, isn’t it. I’m never opening a theatre in December ever again. 

#### CHARLIE  

How long are you here? 

#### JACK  

Just through the end of the month. I like to oversee things still, make sure each new theatre gets off the ground before letting it run on its own. 

#### CHARLIE  

A guiding hand for all your little children. 

#### JACK   

Oh god, I hope this is the closest I ever get to having children. 

#### CHARLIE   

Really? You’ve never wanted to start a family? 

#### JACK  

I think there was a time when I thought I would. It’s the done thing, isn’t it? 

#### CHARLIE  

Mm.

#### JACK  

Though not for us, not anymore. 

#### CHARLIE  

Yes, I’ve had that thought quite often. 

#### JACK  

I can’t say I mourn the loss too terribly. I think wanting to avoid the matter completely made me realize that I never really desired it in the first place. But you aren’t supposed to say that. 

#### CHARLIE  

Nor are you supposed to be a perpetual bachelor and yet here we are. 

#### JACK   

Yes, we don’t have to follow convention, certainly not around each other. _[then]_ But what’s your view? 

#### CHARLIE  

On what? 

#### JACK  

Children. A family. 

#### CHARLIE  

Oh. Well, I suppose I always thought I’d have one, just as you say. 

#### JACK   

And now? Freed from the shackles of expectation? 

#### CHARLIE  

More like imprisoned by unceasing time. 

#### JACK   

You do mourn the loss then? 

#### CHARLIE  

I think I would have liked to be a father. Despite being paltry in the experience of having one myself. 

#### JACK 

I can’t say that you’re particularly bereft for it. 

#### CHARLIE  

Have you spoken to your family recently? 

#### JACK  

Well, talking of my father actually, he’s dead. 

#### CHARLIE  

Oh, John, I’m sorry to hear that. I had no idea. 

#### JACK   

Why would you? 

#### CHARLIE  

Well, I would have thought...

#### JACK  

Yes, he would be the type to have a grand Manhattan funeral, wouldn’t he? He did, as a matter of fact. I went, but didn’t send notice to anyone. 

#### CHARLIE  

I would have come. 

#### JACK   

That’s exactly why I didn’t send notice. It was bad enough I had get myself in gray powder and make-up just to see my family, there was no good reason for you to have to do the same. 

#### CHARLIE  

I don’t. Not anymore. There’s no one left that really knew me then. I’m certain your siblings wouldn’t have remembered me from a midsummer party years ago. 

#### JACK   

Mm, perhaps not. _[beat]_ It just didn’t seem a worthwhile use of your time. I’m not sure it was a worthwhile use of my time. 

#### CHARLIE  

You really despised him that much? 

#### JACK  

Not toward the end. These last few decades, I’m not sure I felt much of anything for him at all. And I only started speaking to the man again a few years ago. _[beat]_ That’s terrible, isn’t it? I trust you won’t think me a monster for it. 

#### CHARLIE  

No, I don’t think you a monster. 

#### JACK   

You pity me, though. 

#### CHARLIE  

No, I don’t. 

#### JACK 

Yes, you do. I see it on your face. I know that look well. 

#### CHARLIE  

What look? 

#### JACK   

The look you get when you’re pitying me. 

#### CHARLIE 

There is no such look. 

#### JACK  

Oh yes there is. It’s been a number of years since I’ve seen it, but it’s just the same as it ever was. “Oh no, poor John, he humiliated himself at the World’s Fair.” “Oh no, poor John, his new act has failed.” “Oh no, poor John, he got himself blown up.” 

#### CHARLIE  

“Oh no, poor John, he got another drink thrown in his face”...?

#### JACK   

Just so. 

#### CHARLIE  

I don’t pity you. _[then]_ And if memory serves, you told me just the same once and I did you the courtesy of believing you. You may as well return the favor. 

#### JACK   

Then explain to me what the emotion is, because it is distinct.

#### CHARLIE 

I...feel sympathy for you. 

#### JACK 

Ha! That’s just another way to say the same thing. 

#### CHARLIE  

No, it isn’t. I’ve never felt bad for you, not really. Nearly everything that’s gone wrong in your life has been because of choices you’ve made yourself. 

#### JACK  

Ah, so you think me a fool then 

#### CHARLIE   

_[sighing]_ I only mean you’re not some poor helpless kitten I’ve found in the rain. I can feel sympathy for you and not want to bundle you into my coat and take you home. 

#### JACK  

You don’t want to bundle me into your coat and take me home? #### CHARLIE    You’re not a pitiable, defenseless creature is all I’m saying. #### JACK    Yes, I believe I see your point. 

#### CHARLIE  

My father was a tough man, had expectations of me I’m certain would have been disappointed had he lived to see me forge ahead in my own life. And I can imagine how that might have soured me against the very idea of fathers. I can understand your position. 

#### JACK  

What were his expectations? 

#### CHARLIE   

That I’d make something of myself. Rise above the station I was born into. 

#### JACK  

And your father would have been disappointed by who you became? My God, I thought my old man was hard to please. 

#### CHARLIE  

Oh, I’m sure he would’ve been eventually happy with the money and the houses and the well-bred acquaintances - present company very much included, I’ve no doubt he would’ve been thrilled to be associated with your family - but it would’ve come on the heels of years full of disdain for my chosen profession. Magic would have struck him as singularly unserious. 

#### JACK   

Even if it brought you riches and renown? 

#### CHARLIE  

The wrong kind of renown in his view. Being known for being a clown is worse than not being known at all. 

#### JACK   

You’re not a clown. 

#### CHARLIE  

Any kind of artistic performance was clownery to my father. 

#### JACK 

He does indeed sound tough. 

#### CHARLIE   

No tougher than yours, I should think. If tough in a different way. 

#### JACK   

Mm. _[then]_ You know, after the funeral...despite the fact that I’d spent years with no attachment to him at all and I’d long given up any hope of meeting his expectations, I still anticipated feeling rudderless in his absence. There is something motivating about going after a goal you know you’ll never reach. 

#### CHARLIE  

Surely the last forty years have not been about seeking your father’s approval? 

#### JACK   

Apparently not. Which is very strange to me, because in my heart of hearts, I somewhat expected that they were. I can claim lack of feeling and my own bold autonomy from my family as much as I want, but that doesn’t mean it’s true. I shoved down the feeling of wanting their love for so long, stuffed it away in a little box that when I opened that box again after he died, I found it was empty. I’d come to believe my own illusion, just as you’ve always accused me of doing. 

#### CHARLIE  

Well, you are a very good illusionist. I’ve always said that too. 

#### JACK  

Not always. 

#### CHARLIE 

No, I suppose not. 

#### JACK   

The question then becomes: what do we do it all for? 

#### CHARLIE   

Pardon?

#### JACK  

Here I’d thought I was secretly driven by a need it turns out I didn’t have, and now I know it isn’t spurring me on, I have to wonder what is. 

#### CHARLIE  

You love the theatre, don’t you? 

#### JACK  

Yes, yes, I do, but I’m not speaking only of ambition and legacy. It’s about the larger picture. 

#### CHARLIE  

The larger picture of what? 

#### JACK   

Of life. And our endless supply of it. 

#### CHARLIE  

I’m not sure I know what you mean. 

#### JACK  

Do you ever feel as if you’re simply passing the time? 

#### CHARLIE  

I- I try not to think of it too much anymore. I try to stay focused on the present moment. 

#### JACK   

Which is why you’re meticulously planning how to mete out all your earnings to whichever good cause can best use it. 

#### CHARLIE  

Planning practical matters and staying in the present moment are not necessarily at odds. And how do you know I’m giving it a good cause? I could be spending it on absurd luxuries. 

#### JACK  

I know you’ve been wealthy for decades now, Charles, but it hasn’t changed you that much. Which cause is it this time? 

#### CHARLIE  

I’m thinking of starting an apprenticeship program for aspiring chemists. 

#### JACK   

What, here? Why not New York? 

#### CHARLIE  

Why are you opening a second theater here in Chicago? 

#### JACK  

I already have three in New York. 

#### CHARLIE 

Exactly. I’ve given plenty to New York throughout the years and Chicago...

#### JACK  

Chicago is where it all began. 

#### CHARLIE  

My birth city in many ways. Yours too.

#### JACK  

Do you think about that day much? 

#### CHARLIE  

In ‘93 you mean? 

#### JACK   

No the other time we performed real, actual magic in Chicago.

#### CHARLIE  

For the last time, John, it wasn’t 

#### JACK  

Real magic, I know you still believe that 

#### CHARLIE  

Well, having never witnessed another inexplicable act in the intervening forty years, I’m not inclined to change my mind 

#### JACK 

And I’m not inclined to have this conversation again. 

#### CHARLIE  

Nor am I. 

#### JACK  

Then good. 

#### CHARLIE  

Good. 

#### JACK  

So you do think about it? 

#### CHARLIE  

Yes. Not as often as I used to but for as much as I try not to think about the future, I can’t always help myself when ruminating on the past. _[then]_ How often do you think of it? 

#### JACK   

More, recently. 

#### CHARLIE  

Really? 

#### JACK

I...you might find this strange, but I miss it. 

#### CHARLIE 

Miss performing? I don’t think that’s strange. 

#### JACK  

No, not performing--although, yes, that too, I suppose. But I know I can always get back on stage when I want to. No, I miss that feeling. 

#### CHARLIE  

What feeling? 

#### JACK  

The one I had when we were preparing the show. When we were doing the show. The one I had in the moment I nearly died.

#### CHARLIE  

You miss brushing up against death? You’re right, I do find that strange. 

#### JACK  

It isn’t that exactly. I don’t miss the war 

#### CHARLIE   

I should hope not 

#### JACK 

I don’t miss wondering if I would die there, I don’t miss the blinding pain of having my flesh torn apart. It isn’t - I’m not flirting with death, if that’s what you’re thinking. 

#### CHARLIE   

That’s not what I’m thinking. 

#### JACK  

I miss the uncertainty. The unpredictability of doing a dangerous stunt, the thrill of it going wrong and surviving it. 

#### CHARLIE  

If memory serves, “thrill” is not what you were experiencing at the time. You were madder than a wet cat. 

#### JACK 

I miss working with you. 

#### CHARLIE 

I- well, I really find that strange. 

#### JACK  

You wouldn’t team up for another world’s fair if we had the chance? 

#### CHARLIE 

That’s not what I’m saying but, John, we were miserable most of the time. Constantly at each other’s throats, never agreeing on any one thing 

#### JACK  

I remember it differently. We made each other better. 

#### CHARLIE  

Both things can be true. _[then, sighing]_ I think- well, we didn’t know each other all that well back then, did we? That was part of the allure, I imagine. Working with someone you hardly knew, never being able to predict their next move. I don’t think we’d have the same experience now. 

#### JACK   

You know me that well, do you? 

#### CHARLIE  

Certainly better than anyone else alive on this earth. 

#### JACK   

I’m moving to California. 

#### CHARLIE  

What?

#### JACK  

The motion pictures, I- well, I want to get in while it’s still relatively early. I really do think it’s sticking around. 

#### CHARLIE   

And you have to go to California to do it? 

#### JACK  

The future is in the City of Angels, haven’t you heard? Seems like the whole city is lit up with spotlights from dusk until dawn. 

#### CHARLIE  

Sounds ill-suited for restful sleep. 

#### JACK  

I can sleep when I’m dead. 

#### CHARLIE  

John 

#### JACK 

I’m not so predictable after all, am I? 

#### CHARLIE 

I suppose not. _[beat]_ Don’t tell me you’ve decided in this moment to move to California simply because I suggested you’d become 

#### JACK  

No, no, of course not. I’ve been thinking about it for a while. 

#### CHARLIE  

When- when do you go? 

#### JACK  

As I said, I’m here until the end of the month and then... 

#### CHARLIE   

And the Long Island house? 

#### JACK   

Keeping it, for the time being. You’re welcome to use it if you get bored of your sprawling penthouse. 

#### CHARLIE  

I- _[then]_ I’m not sure I’ll stay in New York as a matter of fact.

#### JACK 

You’ll move here full-time then? 

#### CHARLIE   

Not necessarily. Once the apprenticeships are set up, I don’t plan on running the thing. 

#### JACK   

So where would you go? 

#### CHARLIE  

South, maybe. Washington, D.C. Get back into government work and make an honest living again.

#### JACK  

You’ll have to find something other than government work then. 

#### CHARLIE  

I’m...concerned. 

#### JACK  

About Hoover? 

#### CHARLIE  

I’m not sure he’s the man for the job. 

#### JACK  

Well it was certainly never going to be Smith. 

#### CHARLIE   

I know that, it was a long shot to be certain but...I’m not sure that we should be so closed off from the rest of the world. It’s been a decade since the war ended and things are still in shambles in Europe and Hoover 

#### JACK   

Ah, I see, you want to go to DC to change his mind, is that it? 

#### CHARLIE  

I want to see if I could maybe be a positive influence. 

#### JACK   

When did you decide this? _[beat]_ Charles, did you decide this just now because I told you I’m moving to California? 

#### CHARLIE  

Of course not. It’s been on my mind for some time and this conversation is merely reminding me 

#### JACK  

You weren’t being glib when you said you like to stay in the present moment, were you? My word. 

#### CHARLIE  

I think I’m sick of New York. 

#### JACK   

A real feat considering you spent the better part of the last decade traveling around the globe. 

#### CHARLIE   

If anything, that made me more sick of New York. It’s one thing to stay in a place for weeks or months at a time, it’s quite another to have a place you call home. 

#### JACK   

And New York isn’t home? 

#### CHARLIE  

I’m not sure it can be anymore, no. 

#### JACK 

I think New York will always be my home, whether I live there or not.

#### CHARLIE  

Well, you were born there. You hardly lived anywhere else for the first thirty years of your life. 

#### JACK 

And now I’ve seen the great wide world. Your own journeys into which you’ve told me hardly anything about. 

#### CHARLIE   

That isn’t true. I sent you postcards. 

#### JACK  

Yes, occasionally and only ever the most basic fundamentals - “I’m now in Greece, John, I’ve seen the Acropolis.” “This week it’s Nepal, John, quite prolific mountains to be had”. “I don’t care for the food in Norway, John, but the constant daylight is a pleasure”. 

#### CHARLIE 

What more is there to say? 

#### JACK 

Plenty! What was your favorite part? What was the greatest hardship? What did you learn, who did you meet, how did you feel? 

#### CHARLIE  

You want me to put on a one-man revue for you. 

#### JACK 

If it wouldn’t be too much to ask. 

#### CHARLIE  

You always think me far more interesting than I actually am. _[then]_ Though, that’s not true- do you ever marvel at how much things have changed? 

#### JACK  

The only thing that’s truly changed is that I can now admit I have, in fact, always found you interesting. 

#### CHARLIE  

Then you really are a fool. 

#### JACK   

You spent two years traveling to six continents, Charles. 

#### CHARLIE  

Yes, I have an interesting life. That does not, inherently, make me interesting. Other than the obvious. Or, obvious to present company. 

#### JACK   

Your immortality is not the only thing that makes you interesting.

#### CHARLIE  

No? 

#### JACK  

No. 

#### CHARLIE  

You’re not going to elaborate?

#### JACK  

If you wanted me to expound on your various good qualities, you should’ve shown up on time. 

#### CHARLIE 

I think you find me interesting because I can keep up with you. Not everyone can and you actually are interesting, I’m merely reflecting back. 

#### JACK  

You really view yourself as so blank? 

#### CHARLIE  

I don’t crave excitement and unpredictability the way you do. Yes, I enjoy changing things up, seeing new things, learning more about the world, but I’m not good in the face of true excitement. 

#### JACK  

Maybe you just haven’t had the right excitement yet. 

#### CHARLIE  

And nearly dying again and again, that’s the right kind of excitement?

#### JACK  

You’re awfully hung up on that. 

#### CHARLIE  

You’re the one who said you missed the thrill of surviving.

#### JACK 

I haven’t had to survive anything in nearly a decade. 

#### CHARLIE   

So the fire was just a regular Tuesday was it? 

#### JACK  

I was fine. 

#### CHARLIE    

You had damaged lungs, John 

#### JACK  

I had a cough and dry eyes for a week, it was hardly my deathbed. The real tragedy was the loss of the theater. 

#### CHARLIE   

The new building is lovely. 

#### JACK   

You’ve been? 

#### CHARLIE   

Of course. I was kidding earlier, I do actually get to your shows from time to time. 

#### JACK   

How many times have you been in New York the past few years and not told me? 

#### CHARLIE  

What?

#### JACK  

You saw the new theater - you know I’ve been in New York

#### CHARLIE  

Not much. 

#### JACK 

Why haven’t you come out to Long Island in years? 

#### CHARLIE  

I didn’t want to smother you. 

#### JACK  

I have nine bedrooms, I am incapable of being smothered in that house. 

#### CHARLIE  

I felt I was quite an annoyance after the fire. I didn’t want to impose further. 

#### JACK  

_[sighing]_ Oh, Charles, just because I was being a brat when you tried to make me soup doesn’t mean you were an annoyance. It means I was angry about losing my theater and tired of not being able to take a full breath. I’m an absolutely terrible patient, you know this. 

#### CHARLIE    

Still. 

#### JACK  

For a man with such strong convictions and a consistent certitude that he’s right about everything, you so often behave as if you don’t belong in places. Like you’re afraid of being noticed. 

#### CHARLIE  

Afraid? No. Apprehensive? Perhaps. I’ve never been like you, as bold off stage as I am on. That’s part of what I loved about the stage. But now...people noticing too much about me could lead to questions I don’t know how to answer. 

#### JACK  

I’m not people. You don’t have to hide from me. 

#### CHARLIE  

Not that, certainly, but if you notice me too much then- _[he cuts himself off]_ 

#### JACK  

Then what? 

#### CHARLIE  

I fear you’ll be horribly disappointed. You won’t find me interesting anymore. 

#### JACK  

Pull the other one. 

#### CHARLIE  

I’m serious.

#### JACK 

What is it you’re so worried you’ll reveal? _[sighing]_ In any case, you weren’t an annoyance. I was grateful to have your assistance after the fire. Just as I am always grateful to have your assistance. 

#### CHARLIE   

Well, then, the next time you get yourself into mortal danger, I’ll be there to offer it again. _[then]_ Though, I would prefer if you didn’t. Put yourself in mortal danger, that is. 

#### JACK  

I suppose I’ll have to find my unpredictability elsewhere. 

#### CHARLIE   

California, perhaps? 

#### JACK   

To start. And if not that, I’ll continue to try making my own. 

#### CHARLIE   

How do you mean? 

#### JACK    

Magic, Charles. My head is consistently on a swivel looking for ways to do magic - real magic - again. 

#### CHARLIE   

Of course. 

#### JACK  

You could come with me, you know. 

#### CHARLIE  

What? 

#### JACK    

To California. You say you want out of New York, so run as far as you can away from it. Run all the way to the West Coast. 

#### CHARLIE 

With you. 

#### JACK   

Why not? It’s a bit silly, isn’t it? Us living our lives so separately. Seeing each other once every now and then, writing only sporadically. 

#### CHARLIE  

I’ve been better of late. 

#### JACK  

I know. 

#### CHARLIE  

I am sorry for being so negligent when I was abroad. 

#### JACK   

It wasn’t just while you were abroad. 

#### CHARLIE  

I did used to be reliable, I think. I’m not sure what’s happened.

#### JACK   

Yes, you were. Once. 

#### CHARLIE   

I’ll admit, John, I’m quite lost. Going to D.C. to look over Hoover’s shoulder was a whim in the moment 

#### JACK  

I knew it 

#### CHARLIE   

And I do think it might be a good idea, that I could provide some insight, some valuable support, but it’s more that I don’t know what else to do. You wrote to me once that you’d personally stagnated and I fear I’ve...spiritually stagnated. As if I’m not really in the world at all. 

#### JACK   

What an awful feeling. 

#### CHARLIE   

It is. 

#### JACK 

There must be a remedy for that, though. I’m certain I’ve felt exactly that way before but the truth is I don’t remember. That’s the thing about a long life--the good and bad bits come and go and at the end of the day, you mostly just remember the good bits. 

#### CHARLIE   

Is that true? You don’t remember the bad bits? 

#### JACK  

Some of them I do. Though I try not to linger on them. 

#### CHARLIE   

I think maybe that’s all I do. Linger. 

#### JACK 

Therein lies your problem. Stewing on the things you’ve done wrong or the things you haven’t done right is surely a recipe for spiritual stagnation. What’s the good of the present moment if you’re haunted by the errors of the past? 

#### CHARLIE 

You’ve become quite wise, you know. 

#### JACK  

I do know, but it’s always nice to hear it from someone else. 

#### CHARLIE   

How did you come by it? The wisdom. 

#### JACK  

I don’t want to say watching hundreds of men die and nearly dying myself...

#### CHARLIE 

But that’s the truth. 

#### JACK

What?

Hm?

The wisdom or the wealth. I have no one to pass it down to.

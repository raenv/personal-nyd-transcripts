##### [A fairly bustling party right after the clock has chimed midnight on New Year’s Eve. The chatter we can hear is mostly that of women, and a gramophone plays over the hubbub]

##### [A tinny version of “Auld Lang Syne” plays. John Fogg calls out to someone as he walks away from the party]

#### JACK   

Yes, in my dressing room- _[laughing]_ Because, darling, you never know when you’ll need a bottle of champagne. I’ll be right back! 

##### [Jack opens his dressing room door and closes it behind him]

Now, where is that bottle... 

##### [He takes a step further inside but is stopped by a knock on the door. He turns around to open it, speaking as he does]

I’ve only been gone for thirty seconds, you’ll get your champagne, you animals- _[then]_ Oh- oh my goodness, Charles Chambers. 

#### CHARLIE   

May I come in? 

#### JACK   

Of course, come in, come in -

##### [Charlie steps inside and Jack closes the door behind him]

What the hell are you doing here? Happy New Year, by the way -

#### CHARLIE   

Happy New Year.

#### JACK   

I was just searching for another bottle of champagne if you didn’t get a chance to toast at midnight- when did you get here? 

#### CHARLIE   

Just now, in fact. And I’m not looking to celebrate. 

#### JACK   

No? 

#### CHARLIE   

I was at a pub not far from here and someone mentioned that you were having a party -

#### JACK   

How strange that you should be just down the street. The world certainly moves in mysterious ways doesn’t it. 

#### CHARLIE   

Not particularly mysterious--I was at your show earlier this evening. 

#### JACK   

You were? Why didn’t you say hello? I had no idea you were even in London at all.

#### CHARLIE   

You were swarmed by admirers, I thought it’d be wise to try another time. 

#### JACK   

How long are you here for? You know, it could be fun to have you join me on stage one evening.

#### CHARLIE   

John -

#### JACK   

Are you sure I can’t get you a drink? I know I have a fairly decent bottle of champagne somewhere around here -

#### CHARLIE   

John, we need to talk. 

#### JACK   

Come on, have a drink with me.

#### CHARLIE   

I - I’ve had plenty tonight, thank you. 

##### [Beat]

#### JACK  

Charles Chambers, are you drunk? 

#### CHARLIE 

Do I sound drunk? 

#### JACK  

No, you don’t, which is very irritating, because you certainly look a little sozzled - glassy eyes, unsteady posture - but of course you would still be able to speak with perfect clarity. 

#### CHARLIE 

Clarity is what brought me here. I’ve been endeavoring to have this conversation with you for some time.

##### [Jack goes over to an armchair and sits down]

#### JACK 

Good god man, you certainly are grim-faced for a man at a party. Sit, sit. 

##### [Charlie sits down]

Now, tell me why you’ve tracked me down, after midnight, inebriated, all to have a conversation. 

#### CHARLIE 

You were right. 

#### JACK 

I know. _[beat]_ About what? 

#### CHARLIE  

_[distraught]_ Something happened to us. Twenty-three years ago, something happened to us in that act. 

#### JACK  

Ah. That. Must we talk about it? 

#### CHARLIE  

Yes, that. What, you work yourself into a froth about it the last time we saw each other -

#### JACK 

It was hardly a froth -

#### CHARLIE  

And now you don’t want to discuss it? 

#### JACK  

I don’t see what there is to discuss. You and I performed a magic trick, something went wrong, and... now we don’t seem to be aging. 

#### CHARLIE

And you don’t know what there is to discuss? Be serious. 

#### JACK  

I am. As far as I can tell, there’s not very much we can do about it except wait and see and enjoy ourselves in the meantime. 

#### CHARLIE  

Enjoy - John, you can’t really be telling me that you think any of this is normal. 

#### JACK  

I didn’t say it was normal, I just don’t think it’s any of our concern. 

#### CHARLIE 

Who’s concern would it be? It only affects us, as far as I can tell, thank god. 

#### JACK  

You’re behaving as if you’re angry about this. 

#### CHARLIE  

_[raising his voice]_ I am! Are you not? 

#### JACK 

Angry about accidentally finding the fountain of youth? Well, no, Charles, I’m not angry about that at all. Why on earth are you? 

#### CHARLIE  

Because it’s unnatural.

#### JACK 

We’re in the business of the unnatural. 

#### CHARLIE 

You are. I’ve given it up. 

#### JACK 

I was wondering about that. I haven’t seen your name on any marquis or theatrical calendar in years. Why? You’re still a young man. 

#### CHARLIE  

No, I’m not! Neither are you. We’re in our fifties. 

#### JACK  

Are we? _[beat]_ Really think about it, Charles, are we actually in our fifties? 

#### CHARLIE  

We’ve been on this Earth for fifty-some-odd years, don’t you think that’s what matters? 

#### JACK  

When the body is still thirty, I don’t think it matters at all how long it’s been walking around. 

#### CHARLIE 

So you don’t think it’s noteworthy that it’s been twenty-three years - nearly a quarter of a century - since either of us has gained a gray hair or new wrinkle. 

#### JACK  

Certainly, it’s noteworthy, I’m just not sure why you’re talking about it like it’s a problem. 

#### CHARLIE

_[upset]_ When does it end? What did we do to ourselves? Are we... _[beat]_

#### JACK  

Many years ago you said you’d be naive to expect my gratitude. 

#### CHARLIE  

Did I? It sounds right, in any case. 

#### JACK 

Perhaps it was once, but whether you expect it or not, you have it. My gratitude. 

#### CHARLIE  

Whatever for? 

#### JACK 

In our last conversation, I accused you of sabotaging my career.

#### CHARLIE  

I know, I remember.

#### JACK  

But you may have given me a great gift after all. My career has never been better and it seems I have unlimited time in which to improve upon it.

#### CHARLIE  

You don’t think that’s ill-advised? 

#### JACK  

What do you mean? 

#### CHARLIE  

Only that - well, this was precisely why I came here tonight and why I’ve hemmed and hawed over reaching out to you in the first place. I wasn’t certain how wise it would be for us to be seen together. 

#### JACK  

Hardly a scandal for two conjurers to be in the same room, especially ones who have worked together. 

#### CHARLIE   

But might it not draw undue attention? It’s one thing for you to flit about from continent to continent doing your shows and having your face on posters when the audiences change, it’s quite another for those audiences to see us side by side. 

#### JACK  

It would be just another illusion. 

#### CHARLIE  

There are some tricks that even the most ardent magic fans will have a hard time justifying as tricks. 

#### JACK   

The very nature of my work puts me on stage, under bright lights, in costume and looking altogether like a character from a melodrama. That’s the entire point. No one is paying any mind to my youth. 

#### CHARLIE  

Are you certain about that? You’ve never received any comments whatsoever in the last two decades? Because I have. 

#### JACK 

The odd compliment here and there, the occasional expression of wonderment. But it only serves to add to the mystique I’ve worked so hard to cultivate. 

#### CHARLIE 

It seems you’ve done a good job of it. Fogg the Fearless becoming the Great and Mysterious Fogg known to all from San Francisco to Berlin. 

#### JACK  

Even my given name fits into the persona. 

#### CHARLIE 

And it seems the universe is poised to give you as much fodder to work with as possible. I’m sure your passage on the Titanic added to the mystery. 

#### JACK 

You know about that? 

#### CHARLIE  

Of course. Everybody does.

#### JACK 

I didn’t realize you were paying such close attention to my life. 

#### CHARLIE  

I was paying close attention to the disaster and was taken aback when I saw your name on the manifest. I was just glad to see it on the list of the living. 

#### JACK  

You were? 

#### CHARLIE 

Of course, I’ve never held any ill will toward you, no matter what you might think. _[a pause]_ You know, I... I wrote to you about it. To your old address in New York. I don’t know if you ever received it. 

#### JACK  

No, I didn’t. At that time, I had no permanent address in New York and forwarding my mail from one place to the next was never terribly reliable. 

#### CHARLIE  

You should’ve gotten a post office box like anyone else who travels frequently. 

#### JACK  

Yes, I suppose I should have. What were the contents of this letter? 

#### CHARLIE  

...More or less what I’ve just said. That I heard you were on the ship and was relieved to hear you’d made it off safely. 

#### JACK 

Hm. 

##### [Beat]

#### CHARLIE  

_[sighing]_ Alright, well, that isn’t entirely accurate. I also asked if, now that you were back in the states... you would be open to a meeting. 

#### JACK  

I would’ve been. I’m sorry I never got the letter. Though I did leave the states to come back here a few years later. 

#### CHARLIE 

Why? I’m not sure I’d ever get back on a transatlantic vessel after what you experienced. 

#### JACK  

We do what we have to for our art, don’t we? And I was offered the residency here, which I could hardly say no to. 

#### CHARLIE  

Even if it brought the possibility of dying out on the cold ocean? 

#### JACK 

I didn’t think of it that way. If anything, being on the most ill-fated journey across the sea made me less anxious about traveling by boat. The Titanic was not the once-in-a-lifetime ship we all hoped she’d be, but her passage was once-in-a-lifetime. When something like that happens - when an endeavor goes so completely, spectacularly, publicly wrong, I believe those who undertook it in the first place are more inclined to get it right the next time they make the attempt. Either that, or they’re people standing on the sidelines, grateful they can learn from other’s mistakes. 

#### CHARLIE  

It’s unlikely to happen again, you mean. 

#### JACK 

It would certainly beggar belief. And it would _really_ be stretching the imagination to think that I would have the bad fortune to experience that twice. 

#### CHARLIE  

_[a small laugh]_ I’m surprised at you. 

#### JACK 

Why? 

#### CHARLIE 

You’ve never been the kind of person who holds statistical probability or logical conclusion above imagination and possibility. 

#### JACK   

You’re saying I’m a fool. 

#### CHARLIE  

No. I’m saying I’m surprised at you. You’ve... changed since I saw you last. 

#### JACK 

Though not in the obvious, physical sense. 

#### CHARLIE 

No. Not in that. 

#### JACK  

I do still put stock in the more whimsical movements of the universe. But I guess you could say I’ve found a balance in my middle age. 

#### CHARLIE  

Hm. How terrifying was it, really? Being there when it went down.

##### [A long beat]

#### JACK  

It was awful. Truly awful. Truthfully, I’m uncertain how I survived. 

#### CHARLIE 

Thank goodness for lifeboats, I suppose. 

#### JACK  

That’s just the thing. I wasn’t in one. Of course I wasn’t in one - I’m a young, fit man, I wasn’t about to take a seat from the female, the young, or the old. 

#### CHARLIE 

You weren’t in a lifeboat? How did you survive?

#### JACK 

This is exactly what I’m trying to say--it feels like a miracle. I kept myself afloat with a bit of debris from the ship - could have been the very dining table I’d eaten at that evening, who knows - and then simply had to... wait. 

#### CHARLIE  

There’s no simply about it, the water must have been freezing. 

#### JACK 

It was. But my body took forever to get too cold to bear and by that time, help was nearly upon us. 

##### [Beat]

#### CHARLIE  

Do you think-_[he stops himself]_ I mean to say, do you think that...

#### JACK 

Do I think that the same error in nature that keeps us young kept me alive? I have no idea. I do know that I’m happy for whatever it is that kept me warm that night and while that was certainly not a trip I’d want to repeat, I’ve thoroughly enjoyed every transatlantic journey I’ve taken since. And at least I did get quite the story out of it. And, as you said - it adds to the mystique. 

#### CHARLIE 

Mister Fogg, you do astound me. 

#### JACK 

I do? 

#### CHARLIE  

Your insistence on optimism and perseverance in the face of all is remarkable. It’s the trait I’ve always most admired about you. 

#### JACK  

What about you? Any particularly harrowing moments from the last decade? 

#### CHARLIE 

Oh, just the usual horrors of life. Love and loss and money, woes and feast and famine, and everything else that makes up our human existence. 

#### JACK 

Love and loss? 

#### CHARLIE 

My mother died. 

#### JACK 

I’m very sorry to hear that. 

#### CHARLIE  

Thank you. I know you never had a close relationship with your parents.

#### JACK  

You have no idea what my parentage is, you mean.

#### CHARLIE 

Yes, I guess I do mean that. I suppose I always assumed that if you did have parents still - or a connection to them - I’d know who they are. 

#### JACK  

That is how New York society works. 

#### CHARLIE 

Exactly. 

#### JACK 

No, you’re right, I don’t have a relationship with them. You, in fact, may know who they are, not that they’re the cream of society or anything, but I have not been considered a son of theirs for a very long time. They were perfectly ordinary parents but with expectations I had no interest in filling. Because of that, any connection was severed long ago. 

#### CHARLIE  

I’m sorry. 

#### JACK  

I’m not. They’re not important to me. They could be dead for all I know, I’ve never lingered on the past. 

#### CHARLIE  

I see. 

#### JACK  

_[stammering]_ That’s not to say you shouldn’t be lingering on your mother’s death -

#### CHARLIE  

Calm yourself, Mister Fogg, I don’t take offense. It was several years ago now, but it... she was my only remaining family. My only true friend in the world, if that isn’t too pitiable to say. 

#### JACK  

I don’t think it is. 

#### CHARLIE   

_[quiet]_ That’s kind of you, even if it isn’t the truth. I know I’m not terribly sociable and lord knows I could make a more concerted effort, but I have trouble with it. With keeping in touch, with making true connections with other people. My mother was the one person on this earth who cared what happened to me and I’ve been somewhat at an odd end since she passed. 

#### JACK  

But how about love? 

#### CHARLIE  

Excuse me? 

#### JACK  

You said love and loss. I take it someone special did enter your life after all? 

#### CHARLIE 

_[choked up]_ Ah. I may have misspoken. A turn of phrase more than anything else. My god, I really am pitiable.

#### JACK  

I don’t pity you. 

#### CHARLIE 

No, you wouldn’t. 

#### JACK 

What does that mean? 

#### CHARLIE  

You’ve always seen my life through rose-colored glass. My career, my wealth, my lifestyle. 

#### JACK  

And why shouldn’t I? You’ve had immense success, which has given you riches and a comfortable life. 

#### CHARLIE 

Can you not say the same for yourself? 

#### JACK  

Now. Now I could. But it took me more than twenty years to truly build to this point. You had all of this back when we were in our thirties - when we were actually in our thirties. 

#### CHARLIE  

You must realize you can’t keep it going for another twenty. 

#### JACK  

And why not? You’re so concerned with someone uncovering the truth of my life, but what truth is there to uncover? That I’m aging unnaturally, slowly? That I may live for many more decades to come? 

#### CHARLIE   

Do you hear yourself? The notion is absurd. If people were to understand that it’s real -

#### JACK   

They won’t. The precise reason you always believed magic wasn’t real, has always, to me, been proof that it is. The people who witness it don’t know what it is.

#### CHARLIE   

People are not good at keeping secrets. If they saw - 

#### JACK  

And I never suggested that the true use of magic was some grand conspiracy. I’ve never believed that there were millions of people running around performing true acts of magic. 

#### CHARLIE  

And yet you believe it’s real. 

#### JACK   

Don’t you? How else would you explain our current state? 

#### CHARLIE   

What happened to us wasn’t magic. It was science. We just don’t understand it yet.

#### JACK 

And perhaps we never will. _[beat]_ Because it’s magic. 

#### CHARLIE  

And we’re the only people in centuries to successfully cross the line from illusion into true enchantment. 

#### JACK   

I think it more likely that the magic people have stumbled upon in the past has been subtler. And where you believe that any true magic would be immediately recognized by anyone, I think that most people are inclined to explain the unexplainable away in their heads, even if the explanation is unsatisfactory. 

#### CHARLIE  

All it takes is one curious person to bring your life down around you. 

#### JACK  

It’s the twentieth century, we’re not burning witches at the stake anymore. 

#### CHARLIE 

I can think of several world governments, not to mention every robber baron on the planet, who would very much like to know how we did what we did. 

#### JACK  

But to your point, we don’t know what we did. Will we wake up one day old men? Are we immortal? Can we be killed? We don’t have the answers to any of these questions. 

##### [a pause]

#### CHARLIE  

Doesn’t that terrify you, John?

#### JACK   

No more than any of the questions that plagued my existence before. At least now I have the luxury of time to search for answers. 

#### CHARLIE 

So we assume. 

#### JACK  

So we assume. 

#### CHARLIE  

Perhaps... 

#### JACK  

Yes? 

#### CHARLIE 

I don’t think it wise for us to socialize publicly too much. 

#### JACK 

Alright... 

#### CHARLIE 

Maybe you’re correct in thinking that most people will see your youthful face and chalk it up to stage makeup or explain it away as another one of your illusions, but to see us both together, my face being known still as it is... I don’t think it wise.

#### JACK 

I wasn’t suggesting we start a new show together. _[beat]_ In fact, I didn’t suggest we socialize at all. We certainly have never been in the habit. 

##### [another pause]

#### CHARLIE  

Right. Yeah, of course. 

#### JACK  

No, go on, what were you going to say? It sounded like you were leading up to a point. 

#### CHARLIE  

I don’t think we should be seen together.

#### JACK   

As you’ve stated many times.

#### CHARLIE 

But perhaps we should...keep in touch. 

#### JACK 

Is this your way of asking for my current mailing address? You can always send letters to the theater, many people do. 

#### CHARLIE 

_[dry]_ How lovely to be relegated to the tier of your most ardent fans. 

#### JACK  

That isn’t what I meant. 

#### CHARLIE  

If you don’t want to give me your address, I won’t be offended - 

#### JACK   

Good god, man, you can have my address, I was - _[a frustrated sigh; then]_ I think keeping in touch is a good idea. If anything changes with the... state of your person, I would like to know. 

#### CHARLIE  

And same for you. 

#### JACK  

Then it’s decided, we’ll endeavor to write each other. 

#### CHARLIE  

Good. 

#### JACK 

Good. 

#### CHARLIE

It might behoove us to check in physically from time to time - in person, that is. 

#### JACK  

What do you propose? I don’t have any plans to leave England soon and I can’t imagine you - well, what are you doing here? You still haven’t said. If you’re not performing anymore...

#### CHARLIE  

I wanted to... I’m not sure, I wanted to do something. 

#### JACK

About the war, you mean? 

#### CHARLIE  

So many are dying and yet back home you’d think there was nothing going on at all. _[beat]_I suppose that isn’t fair. People aren’t burying their heads in the sand, but it all feels... very far away. 

#### JACK  

What is it you’re hoping to accomplish here? You’re not a British citizen. 

#### CHARLIE  

No, I’m not. But when I was touring Europe all those years ago, I made some - well, I hesitate to call them friends - but friendly acquaintances in British politics. And I have... a skill set that I was hoping might be valuable to them. 

#### JACK  

While I can hardly cast stones, seeing as I’m sitting in a theater having just entertained the British public as I do every night, I’m not sure how much the King’s army could benefit from illusion. Unless you’re telling me you want to join my show after all. 

#### CHARLIE  

_[scoffs]_ Hardly. 

#### JACK  

I’m sure you think me incredibly frivolous for having a show in London at all in this particular moment. But people need - 

#### CHARLIE 

People need joy. I understand it perfectly. I don’t think it frivolous. Though I’m sure you’re engaging in much frivolity yourself - I’ve never seen an audience so predominantly female before. You must be having yourself a grand time. 

#### JACK  

That is mainly due to the war than any of my particular charms but you’re right... after all, I need joy too. 

#### CHARLIE 

_[a beat]_ Indeed. 

#### JACK 

So is that the service you’d provide to the war effort? Joy? 

#### CHARLIE 

You underestimate me, Mister Fogg. It isn’t illusion that I want to offer to the cause, but my rather extensive experience in explosives. 

#### JACK  

Ah yes. That. I forgot you were a chemist before you were a magician.

#### CHARLIE  

Only chronologically. Once I realized I could use my knowledge of chemistry to create illusions, my interest in the profession on its own waned. 

#### JACK  

And now? If the Brits don’t have any interest in your creative capabilities to blow things up, will you become a professional chemist again? 

#### CHARLIE 

I’m not sure. I don’t think so. It’s certainly been too many decades and I was only an assistant back then. I wouldn’t know where to start. _[beat]_ No, I’m not sure what I’ll do. 

#### JACK 

Wait another ten years and you could most likely go right back into conjuring with a new name and with audiences none the wiser. 

#### CHARLIE  

Is that your plan? Or will you be the Great and Mysterious Fogg for time in memoriam. 

#### JACK

Who knows. We’ll just have to wait and see... Speaking of which, back on task: these in person gatherings you suggest. 

#### CHARLIE 

It might be prudent. 

#### JACK  

I think it’s a good idea. The question is of when and how often. The where we can decide through correspondence. 

#### CHARLIE 

Can we not decide the other details as well? 

#### JACK  

We could. But given your own self-described inability to keep in touch, I suggest we make a commitment now, to something. 

#### CHARLIE 

Alright. 

#### JACK  

_[a small laugh]_ I’ve just realized - New Year’s. You and I always seem to meet at New Year’s, don’t we? 

#### CHARLIE 

Yes, I guess we do. 

#### JACK 

New Year’s Day is as good as any. Something appropriately cyclical. 

#### CHARLIE  

Do you know, thinking back on that last New Year’s party we were at... in some ways it feels like yesterday, doesn’t it? 

#### JACK  

In some ways.

#### CHARLIE  

The world is so different now than it was but... but... 

#### JACK  

We’re not. 

#### CHARLIE 

We’re not. _[beat]_ Time has been moving differently for me these past few years. 

#### JACK

I know what you mean. 

#### CHARLIE  

An ordinary relic of getting older I know - time passing you by on swift wings. 

#### JACK

But it’s worse when you don’t see it in your own reflection. _[beat]_ Or, maybe not worse. More pronounced. 

#### CHARLIE 

Exactly. 

#### JACK  

So, not yearly meetings. Not enough time to demonstrably measure change. _[beat]_ I propose every thirteen years. Every thirteen years on New Year’s Day, we’ll meet, compare notes, share news about the world, and be each other’s measuring stick for how much we’ve aged. 

#### CHARLIE 

Very well. It’s decided then. 

##### [Charlie stands up, Jack following]

#### JACK  

You’re leaving? 

#### CHARLIE 

I’ve kept you from your own celebration long enough. 

#### JACK  

You know, you can stay, celebrate with us. 

#### CHARLIE 

No. I got what I came for. 

#### JACK  

And what was that? 

##### [Beat]

#### CHARLIE  

Some assurance I hadn’t gone insane. 

#### JACK 

_[laughs]_ You - you haven’t gone insane.

##### [Charlie walks to the door]

#### CHARLIE  

Enjoy the rest of your party. 

#### JACK   

Thank you. Good luck with the explosives business. 

#### CHARLIE  

Thank you. 

##### [Charlie opens the door and then pauses]

#### CHARLIE 

Why every thirteen? 

#### JACK  

We’re magicians, thirteen feels appropriate. 

#### CHARLIE 

We’re not magicians anymore. At least, I’m not. And I urge you to take my advice - the longer you keep up this performing business, the more likely it is you’ll draw unwanted attention. 

#### JACK  

That may be the case, and maybe a day will come when neither of us will ever perform illusions ever again. But we’ll always be magicians, Charles. ‘Till the day we die. Whenever that might be. Goodbye, Mister Chambers. 

#### CHARLIE  

Goodbye, Mister Fogg. 

##### [Charlie walks through the door, leaving Jack behind in his dressing room]

‘Till the day we die.

##### [a letter received by Reginald F. Sterling, a financier in London]

#### November 19th, 1915

Dear Reg, 

I think you’ll be pleased to know that I’m currently smack dab in the middle of the Atlantic. That’s right—I’m on my way back to London, this time to stay for quite the duration. 

I so enjoyed the time we spent together when I was across the pond all those years ago and it has been a great pleasure in the intervening time to have you as a close and frequent correspondent. You are exceptionally good company and company I would like to keep at every possibility. 

I’ll be keeping residence in St. John’s Wood and would adore having you as a guest. You may find me at the return address on this letter. 

I won’t be able to post this until I’ve arrived in Liverpool and by then I’ll be mere hours away from knocking on your door. But until that time I am, as ever,

Yours truly, 

John

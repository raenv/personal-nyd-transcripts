##### [a letter received by M. P. Coughlin]

#### June 3rd, 1897

Dear Mother, 

I am coming home. Next month, your son is returning to the United States after years aboard, with stories to tell and souvenirs aplenty. I go right back into performances in New York and would like to invite you to stay with me in the City, perhaps even take in my show. I know the trip to Europe was too far and too difficult, but there are so many ways to get to New York from home now and I will cover all expenses. I have become quite successful in both America and Europe, and it would mean a great deal to me if you made the journey. 

Either way, I will see you in August when I have a break in shows and can make the trip to Cincinnati. 

I’m sending money to your accounts as usual, though there is a little extra this time.  The theatre in Barcelona added several shows and I had not accounted for the additional funds in my own budget. I know you can make much better use of it than I ever could. 

I hope you are enjoying the beginning of summer and I am, 

Your loving son,

Charlie

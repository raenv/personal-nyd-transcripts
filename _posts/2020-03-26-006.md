##### [a letter received by M. P. Coughlin]

#### April 17th, 1895

Dear Mother,

I have made it safely to Switzerland. You would not believe the beauty of the Swiss Alps, but I hope this postcard provides you some idea. Once again, I ask you to consider coming over to join me on part of my tour. All the venues are providing more than ample accommodations and the people of the continent could not be kinder.

I understand your reluctance to spend so much time at sea, but I will pay for the finest cabin on the finest ship. I simply wish you would venture from the bounds of Ohio. There is so much to see.

Please consider it. I am,

Your loving son,

Charlie

##### [from the personal diary of J.S. Fogg]

#### December 31st, 1902

It feels particularly appropriate that I should end the year with another rejection from a theater. Part of me wonders why I even bother. 

The act is excellent, I know it is. And it works. It works beautifully, I’ve smoothed out all the kinks and perfected the timing. The sole issue is that no one seems interested in letting me mount it. Again and again, venues cite the difficulty of the equipment the act requires or the fear that I will somehow set their whole theater ablaze or perhaps kill myself on stage. 

It’s that damned World’s Fair act, yet again. It has haunted me ever since that day. All that people seem to remember about it is the one night it failed, not the dozens of nights that we performed it to roaring applause. 

I’m going to Hayward’s New Year’s party tonight, I suppose because I am a glutton for punishment. But he always invites the most influential people in theater—and everything else—that I feel it must be worth it to attend and at least attempt to sniff out potential investors. 

And, who knows, maybe I’ll see Charles Chambers. I’d certainly like to give him a piece of my mind.
